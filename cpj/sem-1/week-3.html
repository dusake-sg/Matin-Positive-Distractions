Visiting Hospitals

Are readings enough to begin an action plan?

I don’t understand how going to hospitals will enhance my understanding of the situation or help with creating the solution when I already have readings from other researchers, online videos and images to cross-reference.

I’ll go to SGH, CGH and KKH since these 3 hospitals were my case studies for last year’s compendium—I think it’s only fair that I visit them physically, and who knows they might help me garner more insights by creating comparisons.

KKH

Visited KKH (with Aditi and Rossane)
Explored level 1 and basement 1. Public access is limited to 2, 1, B1 and B2.
Split into half, the left side is children’s wards, and the right is women’s.
Main hallways are filled with shops and F&Bs (on one side) that are bright and lively.
The other side is where some wards are placed.
Not sure why all waiting rooms on level 1 were empty (at the time of visit).
Some had simple notes that said they’ve moved. Others didn’t and seemed to be open and functioning as per usual—except no patients and family members were inside.

Children’s side had some differentiated design elements like splashes of bright colours and the use of a stylised (childish) font for certain signs.
Some artworks were scattered around. Most walls felt empty.
Lighting for shops was good. Lighting for the wards was dimly lit.
Had to stop the visit short because a cleaning uncle seemed to be wary of us and began tailing.
We left promptly without making a fuss about it.

Insights—Images taken can be split into 3 main areas;
Signs (Main visual element that aids movement)
Artworks (Static visuals that aim to elevate aesthetics/moods)
Spaces (Overall feel of a space based on utility, rate of utilisation and lighting)

End note: Freaking hate fieldwork within a specific building. The last time I tried to photograph/film something was IKEA and that was a hassle too. I should’ve picked something more publicly acceptable and accessible as a project 🤧.
