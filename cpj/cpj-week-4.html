Early Experiments with Tech

Getting Ideas by Experimenting

There's not a whole lot I know about physical computation and as Andreas has iterated multiple times, the best way to learn is by doing.

So let's get building.

Dall.E

Ying Jia was curious to see what fruit stickers would be like to an AI and asked if I knew any good links. Since I was already on the site, I decided to search “digital tech wayfinding in hospital”.

I wasn’t too impressed with what I saw because they simply seemed to imply digital displays and an over-emphasis on the colour blue. But it does inform me of the typical trends around the designs of hospital spaces, what's expected and comfortable.

Symbols

Arduino, LED Panels

Top left: Turning on the LED Panels using online instructions.

Top right: Setup from code to panels to create a moving set of arrows.

Right: Overhead version of electronics setup.

Bottom: Gif of moving arrows in a simulated corridor.

The experiments above helped me discover the Arduino system. I learned that the coding language was similar enough to Javascript that I roughly understood what parameters to play with to create the animated arrows (seen in the images above).
