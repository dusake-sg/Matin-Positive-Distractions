Animal Development
The current concept and experiment feels too literal of a translation from reality. It’d be more interesting to explore aspects of what it means to be aware of the presence of birds or live animals.

I don’t have any immediate ideas now but aspects like bird flocks and rustling leaves from the hops of birds come to mind. Other more urbanised experiences would be pigeons who walk by the street side with their typical head swagger.

Aditi mentions the concept of time that can be perceived from specific bird species and their coos and chirps.

Adversely, a fear of birds are common amongst people and might not be as appreciated as the other aspects. She brings up a good point but maybe there’s something to learn from how people react to an abstract presence of birds.

Greenery Development
I should add in the experiment with the tissue box as a material as it shows the immediacy of making an idea into reality.

Despite the lack of appropriate material, the resourcefulness of making use of what’s around really helps to test whether something works as well as we think it does.
